The Artist's Story

About Adwait

Swami Anand Atirek

28 Years of Devotional Art

In the ancient city of Vadodara, where art flows through every street, Swami Anand Atirek found his calling not in a studio, but in stillness. His journey as an artist began over 28 years ago — not merely as a craft, but as a spiritual practice. Each brushstroke, each carved line, each painted stone is a meditation.

Born of devotion, his art speaks the language of the divine. Whether it is the intricate details of Lord Krishna's eye painted on a river stone, or the sweeping sacred geometry of his digital mandalas, every creation emerges from a state of inner quiet — Adwait, or "non-dual" awareness.

His mastery spans multiple disciplines: traditional canvas painting, pencil and charcoal sketching, pebble and stone art, henna and mehndi, wood carving, and cutting-edge digital tools including Maya animation, 3D modeling, Photoshop, and AutoCAD. He has exhibited at MS University Vadodara and various regional exhibitions.

His philosophy is simple: "Art is not made — it is received. The artist is merely the vessel through which silence finds its form."
